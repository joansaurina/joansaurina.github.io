About Me

My journey did not begin with a fully formed roadmap, but with a fundamental curiosity about the logic of artificial intelligence. I enrolled at the Polytechnic University of Catalonia (UPC) to pursue a Bachelor’s in Artificial Intelligence, driven by a desire to master the mathematical and computational tools that define modern problem-solving.

A pivotal moment in my trajectory was joining Prof. Alexander Ioannidis’ lab at Stanford University. What started as remote collaboration evolved into an immersive residency where I employed deep learning to analyze health data and unravel complex biological patterns.

I am now preparing for my tenure at the European Bioinformatics Institute (EMBL-EBI) in Cambridge, starting in January 2026, where I will expand my scope to drug toxicity using LLMs and Cell Painting data. My ultimate goal is to dissolve the boundaries between human precision medicine and animal health—the essence of the One Health approach.
